# Out of the Tar Pit

**Ben Moseley, Peter Marks** · 2006 · Paper
**Category:** Engineering and Systems → Software Complexity
**Difficulty:** Advanced · **Status:** `reviewed` · **Credibility:** 8/10 · **Usefulness:** 8/10

## Summary
A paper arguing that complexity is the single biggest cause of problems in software, distinguishing essential from accidental complexity and advocating for minimizing mutable state. A favorite of thoughtful engineers.

## Key takeaways
- Most software pain comes from accidental, not essential, complexity.
- State and control flow are primary sources of accidental complexity.
- Functional and declarative approaches can reduce the complexity burden.

## Why it matters
A rigorous articulation of why simplicity is the core engineering virtue.

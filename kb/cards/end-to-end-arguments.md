# End-to-End Arguments in System Design

**J. Saltzer, D. Reed, D. Clark** · 1984 · Paper
**Category:** Engineering and Systems → System Design Principles
**Difficulty:** Advanced · **Status:** `reviewed` · **Credibility:** 10/10 · **Usefulness:** 7/10

## Summary
A classic paper articulating the end-to-end principle: functions are best implemented at the endpoints of a system rather than in its core. It shaped the design of the internet itself.

## Key takeaways
- Push application-specific functions to the endpoints, keep the network simple.
- Explains why the internet's 'dumb core, smart edges' design works.
- A reusable lens for deciding where complexity belongs in any system.

## Why it matters
One of the deepest design principles in computing, and a key reason the internet scaled.

# Computing Machinery and Intelligence

**Alan Turing** · 1950 · Essay
**Category:** AI Foundations → Origins of AI
**Difficulty:** Intermediate · **Status:** `core` · **Credibility:** 10/10 · **Usefulness:** 7/10

## Summary
Turing's seminal essay asking 'Can machines think?' and proposing the Imitation Game (the Turing Test) as an operational substitute for the question. It anticipated and rebutted most objections to machine intelligence still debated today.

## Key takeaways
- Reframes an unanswerable philosophical question into a testable behavioral one.
- Systematically pre-empts the major objections to machine intelligence.
- Foreshadows machine learning by suggesting we build a 'child machine' that learns.

## Why it matters
The intellectual starting point of the entire field of AI, written before the first AI program existed.

# Time, Clocks, and the Ordering of Events in a Distributed System

**Leslie Lamport** · 1978 · Paper
**Category:** Engineering and Systems → Distributed Systems
**Difficulty:** Advanced · **Status:** `recommended` · **Credibility:** 10/10 · **Usefulness:** 7/10

## Summary
A foundational distributed-systems paper introducing logical clocks and the 'happens-before' relation, giving a rigorous way to reason about ordering events without synchronized physical time.

## Key takeaways
- Introduces logical (Lamport) clocks and the happens-before relation.
- Shows you can order distributed events without a shared physical clock.
- Underpins much of modern distributed-systems theory.

## Why it matters
One of the most cited and important papers in computer science; the root of how we reason about distributed time.

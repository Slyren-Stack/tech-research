# As We May Think

## Summary
A 1945 Atlantic essay imagining the 'Memex', a device for storing and linking all of one's books, records, and communications by association — a striking anticipation of hypertext, personal computing, and the web.

## Key takeaways
- Imagined associative information linking decades before hypertext existed.
- Anticipated the personal knowledge machine and the web.
- Framed information overload and augmentation as central challenges.

## Why it matters
The conceptual seed of hypertext and the web, written before computers were programmable as we know them.

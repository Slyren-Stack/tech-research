# The Cathedral and the Bazaar

**Eric S. Raymond** · 1999 · Essay
**Category:** Engineering and Systems → Open Source
**Difficulty:** Beginner · **Status:** `reviewed` · **Credibility:** 8/10 · **Usefulness:** 7/10

## Summary
An influential essay contrasting two development models — the cathedral (closed, top-down) and the bazaar (open, decentralized) — using Linux as the case study. It helped legitimize open-source development to industry.

## Key takeaways
- 'Given enough eyeballs, all bugs are shallow.'
- Open, iterative, community-driven development can outperform closed teams.
- Release early, release often, and treat users as co-developers.

## Why it matters
Shaped the open-source movement and the way modern software is built and shipped.

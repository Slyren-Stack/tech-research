# You and Your Research

**Richard Hamming** · 1986 · Talk
**Category:** Founder Mental Models → Doing Important Work
**Difficulty:** Intermediate · **Status:** `core` · **Credibility:** 9/10 · **Usefulness:** 9/10

## Summary
A famous Bell Labs talk on why some scientists do Nobel-caliber work and others don't. Hamming argues it's less about raw genius and more about courage, working on important problems, and managing yourself.

## Key takeaways
- Work on important problems — most people avoid them.
- Open doors and ambient curiosity compound into great work over years.
- Self-management, courage, and how you handle 'the system' matter enormously.

## Why it matters
A timeless meditation on how to aim a career at work that actually matters.

## Audience fit
Researchers, engineers, founders — anyone ambitious.

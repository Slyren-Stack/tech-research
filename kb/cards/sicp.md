# Structure and Interpretation of Computer Programs (SICP)

## Summary
A legendary MIT textbook that teaches programming as the art of controlling complexity through abstraction. Famous for being hard, deep, and permanently changing how you think about code.

## Key takeaways
- Programming is fundamentally about building and composing abstractions.
- Covers higher-order functions, state, interpreters, and metalinguistic abstraction.
- Rewires how you think more than it teaches any single language.

## Why it matters
Among the most influential CS texts ever written; a rite of passage for serious programmers.

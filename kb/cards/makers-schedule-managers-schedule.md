# Maker's Schedule, Manager's Schedule

**Paul Graham** · 2009 · Essay
**Category:** Founder Mental Models → Productivity & Focus
**Difficulty:** Beginner · **Status:** `recommended` · **Credibility:** 9/10 · **Usefulness:** 8/10

## Summary
A short essay distinguishing the manager's schedule (chopped into hour-long meetings) from the maker's schedule (long uninterrupted blocks), and why meetings are so costly for people who build.

## Key takeaways
- Makers need long, uninterrupted blocks; a single meeting can wreck an afternoon.
- Managers and makers operate on fundamentally different time models.
- Protect deep-work time deliberately, especially in technical teams.

## Why it matters
A small idea that reshapes how builders and their managers should structure time.

## Audience fit
Engineers, designers, and the people who manage them.

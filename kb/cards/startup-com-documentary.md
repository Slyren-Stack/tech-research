# Startup.com

**Chris Hegedus, Jehane Noujaim (dir.)** · 2001 · Documentary

## Summary
A raw, cinema-vérité documentary following two childhood friends as they launch govWorks, a government-focused dot-com, during the internet bubble. It captures the dot-com boom and bust up close, including the strain it puts on the founders' partnership.

## Key takeaways
- An unfiltered, real-time view of a startup's rise and collapse
- Shows how the dot-com bubble's hype and funding distorted decisions
- A case study in how co-founder relationships fracture under pressure

## Why it matters
One of the most honest fly-on-the-wall records of startup failure and the dot-com era, valuable precisely because it isn't a sanitized retrospective.

## Audience fit
Founders, co-founders, anyone studying startup risk and the dot-com era.

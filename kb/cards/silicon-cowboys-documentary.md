## Summary
A documentary on the rise of Compaq, founded in 1982 by three Texas friends — Rod Canion, Jim Harris, and Bill Murto — who built the first IBM-compatible portable PC and took on IBM in the personal computer market, with venture capitalist Ben Rosen backing them.

## Key takeaways
- An underdog challenger can beat an incumbent by out-executing on compatibility and speed
- Compaq's IBM-compatible strategy helped open up the PC clone market
- A vivid case study in 1980s startup ambition and hardware competition

## Why it matters
Tells a foundational and often-overlooked PC-industry story: how a startup challenged IBM and helped shape the open PC ecosystem.

# Startup History

How companies actually got started — first-hand accounts, hard lessons, and the canon of startup thinking.

13 item(s), ranked by signal. ⭐ = core / must-experience.

---

### ⭐ [The Lean Startup](../cards/the-lean-startup.md)
*Eric Ries · 2011 · Book · Beginner*

Introduces build-measure-learn, MVPs, and pivot/persevere thinking for startups and innovative organizations.

**Why it matters:** The defining framework for modern startup experimentation and product iteration.

### ⭐ [The Innovator's Dilemma](../cards/the-innovator-s-dilemma.md)
*Clayton M. Christensen · 1997 · Book · Intermediate*

Explains how successful companies can fail when new technologies start in low-end or overlooked markets.

**Why it matters:** A foundational theory of innovation, strategy, and why new entrants beat incumbents.

### ⭐ [The Hard Thing About Hard Things](../cards/hard-thing-about-hard-things.md)
*Ben Horowitz · 2014 · Book · Intermediate*

A blunt, experience-driven book on the brutal parts of running a company that no one prepares you for: layoffs, near-death moments, demoting friends, and making decisions with no good options.

**Why it matters:** The definitive read on the operational and emotional reality of leading a startup.

### ⭐ [How to Start a Startup (Stanford CS183B)](../cards/how-to-start-a-startup-cs183b.md)
*Sam Altman, Y Combinator, et al. · 2014 · Lecture Series · Beginner*

A free Stanford lecture series organized by Y Combinator, with talks from Sam Altman, Paul Graham, Peter Thiel, Marc Andreessen, and others covering ideas, teams, product, growth, and fundraising.

**Why it matters:** The most comprehensive free startup curriculum, taught by the people who define the field.

### [YC Startup Library](../cards/yc-startup-library.md)
*Y Combinator · 2023 · Library · Beginner*

A consolidated library of startup essays, videos, and talks from YC on how to start and grow companies.

**Why it matters:** A high-signal living library from the most influential startup incubator in the world.

### ⭐ [The Mom Test](../cards/the-mom-test.md)
*Rob Fitzpatrick · 2013 · Book · Beginner*

A compact guide to customer conversations that avoids flattering but useless feedback.

**Why it matters:** One of the most practical tools for real customer learning before you build.

### ⭐ [Zero to One](../cards/zero-to-one.md)
*Peter Thiel with Blake Masters · 2014 · Book · Beginner*

A contrarian set of essays on building valuable companies, arguing that great startups create something new ('zero to one') rather than copying ('one to n'), and that monopolies built on real differentiation drive progress.

**Why it matters:** A widely read framework for thinking about differentiation, monopoly, and ambition.

### [Crossing the Chasm](../cards/crossing-the-chasm.md)
*Geoffrey A. Moore · 2014 · Book · Intermediate*

Describes the gap between early adopters and the mainstream market for breakthrough technology products.

**Why it matters:** Still essential for teams trying to move from niche enthusiasm to broad adoption.

### [Startup School](../cards/startup-school.md)
*Y Combinator · 2022 · Course · Beginner*

YC’s online course for founders, taught by YC partners and successful founders.

**Why it matters:** A practical founder curriculum that compresses years of startup trial-and-error into structured lessons.

### [Founders at Work](../cards/founders-at-work.md)
*Jessica Livingston · 2007 · Book · Beginner*

A collection of candid interviews with founders of major tech companies about their early, messy, uncertain days. It captures what startups actually feel like before they look inevitable.

**Why it matters:** A grounding antidote to startup mythology, from a Y Combinator co-founder.

### [Scaling Up Excellence](../cards/scaling-up-excellence.md)
*Robert I. Sutton, Huggy Rao · 2014 · Book · Intermediate*

A framework for preserving quality while growing teams and organizations.

**Why it matters:** Very useful once a startup begins facing growth-induced friction.

### [The Startup Way](../cards/the-startup-way.md)
*Eric Ries · 2017 · Book · Intermediate*

Extends Lean Startup principles to large organizations and continuous innovation systems.

**Why it matters:** Useful for teams that need startup speed inside a larger company or studio.

### [The Cold Start Problem: How to Start and Scale Network Effects](../cards/cold-start-problem-andrew-chen.md)
*Andrew Chen · Unknown · Talk · Intermediate*

A Talks at Google session with a16z general partner Andrew Chen, based on his 2021 book, on how products overcome the 'cold start problem' — the difficulty of getting a network-effects business off the ground when an empty network has little value — and how to grow network effects from a small working network to a tipping point and beyond.

**Why it matters:** Network effects underlie many of the most valuable tech companies; this is a practical framework for the specific, underrated challenge of starting and scaling them.

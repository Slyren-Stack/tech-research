# Documentary and Long-Form Media

Films and long-form storytelling that capture the culture and history of technology.

13 item(s), ranked by signal. ⭐ = core / must-experience.

---

### [The Computer Chronicles - The Internet (1993)](../cards/the-computer-chronicles-the-internet-1993.md)
*The Computer Chronicles · 1993 · TV episode · Beginner*

A time-capsule view of the early internet before the web became mainstream.

**Why it matters:** Excellent historical context for how quickly internet infrastructure moved from niche to mass market.

### [The Machine That Changed the World - Episode 1: Great Brains](../cards/the-machine-that-changed-the-world-episode-1-great-brains.md)
*WGBH / BBC · 1992 · Documentary episode · Beginner*

The first episode of the classic computer-history documentary series, tracing the earliest eras of computing and key pioneers.

**Why it matters:** One of the best long-form introductions to the pre-personal-computer arc.

### [The Machine That Changed the World - Episode 2: Inventing the Future](../cards/the-machine-that-changed-the-world-episode-2-inventing-the-f.md)
*WGBH / BBC · 1992 · Documentary episode · Beginner*

The sequel episode that moves the story into programming, commercialization, and the road toward personal computing.

**Why it matters:** A strong companion to Episode 1 for seeing how the field became a mass industry.

### [AlphaGo](../cards/alphago.md)
*Greg Kohs (dir.) / DeepMind · 2017 · Documentary · Beginner*

A documentary on DeepMind's AlphaGo defeating Go champion Lee Sedol, including the famous 'Move 37.'

**Why it matters:** A vivid, emotional reference point for what AI surpassing humans actually felt like.

### ⭐ [General Magic](../cards/general-magic-documentary.md)
*Sarah Kerruish, Matt Maude · 2018 · Documentary · Beginner*

A documentary about General Magic, the 1990s Apple spin-off that tried to build the smartphone decades too early. It failed commercially but its alumni went on to shape the iPhone, Android, eBay, and more.

**Why it matters:** A rare, honest film about a brilliant failure that quietly shaped modern tech.

### [The Playbook: Lessons from 200+ Company Stories](../cards/the-playbook-lessons-from-200-company-stories.md)
*Acquired · 2022 · Podcast episode · Intermediate*

A synthesis episode that distills lessons from hundreds of company histories into strategy, product, moats, and management.

**Why it matters:** A compact distillation of the Acquired worldview, useful as an entry point to tech company history.

### [Startup.com](../cards/startup-com-documentary.md)
*Chris Hegedus, Jehane Noujaim (dir.) · 2001 · Documentary · Beginner*

A raw, cinema-vérité documentary following two childhood friends as they launch govWorks, a government-focused dot-com, during the internet bubble. It captures the dot-com boom and bust up close, including the strain it puts on the founders' partnership.

**Why it matters:** One of the most honest fly-on-the-wall records of startup failure and the dot-com era, valuable precisely because it isn't a sanitized retrospective.

### [Silicon Cowboys](../cards/silicon-cowboys-documentary.md)
*Jason Cohen (dir.) · 2016 · Documentary · Beginner*

A documentary on the rise of Compaq, founded in 1982 by three Texas friends — Rod Canion, Jim Harris, and Bill Murto — who built the first IBM-compatible portable PC and took on IBM in the personal computer market, with venture capitalist Ben Rosen backing them.

**Why it matters:** Tells a foundational and often-overlooked PC-industry story: how a startup challenged IBM and helped shape the open PC ecosystem.

### [The Social Network](../cards/the-social-network.md)
*David Fincher (dir.) / Aaron Sorkin · 2010 · Film · Beginner*

The dramatized origin story of Facebook, founder conflict, and the cost of ambition.

**Why it matters:** The most culturally pervasive depiction of startup founding — shared reference for everyone.

### [Something Ventured](../cards/something-ventured-documentary.md)
*Dan Geller, Dayna Goldfine · 2011 · Documentary · Beginner*

A documentary on the birth of the venture capital industry, following the early VCs who backed Apple, Intel, Genentech, Atari, and Cisco, and how their bets created Silicon Valley.

**Why it matters:** The clearest narrative of where venture capital came from and why it mattered.

### [Triumph of the Nerds](../cards/triumph-of-the-nerds.md)
*Robert X. Cringely · 1996 · Documentary · Beginner*

A three-part documentary on the rise of the personal computer industry, from hobbyist garages to Apple, Microsoft, and IBM, told with first-hand interviews of the key players.

**Why it matters:** A lively, primary-source history of the personal computer revolution.

### [Pirates of Silicon Valley](../cards/pirates-of-silicon-valley.md)
*Martyn Burke (dir.) · 1999 · Film · Beginner*

A docudrama on the Apple–Microsoft rivalry and the personalities of Jobs and Gates.

**Why it matters:** A nostalgic touchstone for the founding rivalry that shaped personal computing.

### [The Social Dilemma](../cards/the-social-dilemma.md)
*Jeff Orlowski (dir.) · 2020 · Documentary · Beginner*

A documentary in which former insiders critique social media's attention and manipulation mechanics.

**Why it matters:** The mainstream on-ramp to the social-media-harms debate that most people have seen.

# Engineering and Systems

Systems thinking for builders: distributed systems, software design, and the papers that shaped the field.

16 item(s), ranked by signal. ⭐ = core / must-experience.

---

### ⭐ [Designing Data-Intensive Applications](../cards/designing-data-intensive-applications.md)
*Martin Kleppmann · 2017 · Book · Advanced*

A rigorous, readable book on the fundamentals of storing and processing data at scale: replication, partitioning, consistency, consensus, batch and stream processing. The modern reference for backend and data engineers.

**Why it matters:** The single most recommended systems book for backend engineers in the last decade.

### ⭐ [Refactoring](../cards/refactoring.md)
*Martin Fowler · 1999 · Book / reference · Intermediate*

Defines refactoring as behavior-preserving improvement to the internal structure of code.

**Why it matters:** Essential for any team maintaining code over time.

### [Working Effectively with Legacy Code](../cards/working-effectively-with-legacy-code.md)
*Michael Feathers · 2004 · Book · Advanced*

A practical guide to safely changing existing codebases without rewriting everything.

**Why it matters:** One of the most useful books for teams inheriting messy or fragile systems.

### ⭐ [The Pragmatic Programmer](../cards/the-pragmatic-programmer.md)
*Andy Hunt, Dave Thomas · 2019 · Book · Intermediate*

A modern update to the software craft classic focused on practical, professional programming habits.

**Why it matters:** A durable craftsmanship text for individual developers and small teams.

### [Continuous Delivery](../cards/continuous-delivery.md)
*Martin Fowler · 2013 · Essay / book summary · Intermediate*

Defines continuous delivery as building software so it can be released to production at any time.

**Why it matters:** A key mental model for product teams shipping frequently and safely.

### [Release It! • Michael Nygard & Trisha Gee • GOTO 2023](../cards/release-it-michael-nygard-trisha-gee-goto-2023.md)
*Michael Nygard, Trisha Gee · 2023 · Talk / interview · Advanced*

A production-hardened view of what fails in real systems and how engineers can design for resilience.

**Why it matters:** A must-watch for teams that need to understand the difference between working code and survivable systems.

### [Patterns of Enterprise Application Architecture](../cards/patterns-of-enterprise-application-architecture.md)
*Martin Fowler and collaborators · 2002 · Book / reference · Advanced*

A catalog of enterprise architecture patterns for layering, domain logic, persistence, and web applications.

**Why it matters:** A strong language for discussing how larger software systems are composed.

### [The Mythical Man-Month](../cards/mythical-man-month.md)
*Fred Brooks · 1975 · Book · Beginner*

A classic collection of essays on software engineering and project management, most famous for Brooks's Law: adding manpower to a late software project makes it later. Still painfully relevant decades on.

**Why it matters:** The original wisdom on why software projects slip — still cited because it's still true.

### [Concurrency Is Not Parallelism](../cards/concurrency-is-not-parallelism.md)
*Rob Pike · 2012 · Talk · Intermediate*

A crisp talk by a co-creator of Go drawing the distinction between concurrency (structuring a program as independently executing parts) and parallelism (executing them simultaneously). Clears up a near-universal confusion.

**Why it matters:** A short talk that permanently sharpens how you reason about concurrent software.

### [Time, Clocks, and the Ordering of Events in a Distributed System](../cards/time-clocks-ordering-lamport.md)
*Leslie Lamport · 1978 · Paper · Advanced*

A foundational distributed-systems paper introducing logical clocks and the 'happens-before' relation, giving a rigorous way to reason about ordering events without synchronized physical time.

**Why it matters:** One of the most cited and important papers in computer science; the root of how we reason about distributed time.

### [End-to-End Arguments in System Design](../cards/end-to-end-arguments.md)
*J. Saltzer, D. Reed, D. Clark · 1984 · Paper · Advanced*

A classic paper articulating the end-to-end principle: functions are best implemented at the endpoints of a system rather than in its core. It shaped the design of the internet itself.

**Why it matters:** One of the deepest design principles in computing, and a key reason the internet scaled.

### [Structure and Interpretation of Computer Programs (SICP)](../cards/sicp.md)
*Harold Abelson, Gerald Jay Sussman, Julie Sussman · 1985 · Book · Advanced*

A legendary MIT textbook that teaches programming as the art of controlling complexity through abstraction. Famous for being hard, deep, and permanently changing how you think about code.

**Why it matters:** Among the most influential CS texts ever written; a rite of passage for serious programmers.

### [Out of the Tar Pit](../cards/out-of-the-tar-pit.md)
*Ben Moseley, Peter Marks · 2006 · Paper · Advanced*

A paper arguing that complexity is the single biggest cause of problems in software, distinguishing essential from accidental complexity and advocating for minimizing mutable state. A favorite of thoughtful engineers.

**Why it matters:** A rigorous articulation of why simplicity is the core engineering virtue.

### [Clean Code](../cards/clean-code.md)
*Robert C. Martin · 2008 · Book · Intermediate*

A guide to code readability, naming, structure, and professional programming habits.

**Why it matters:** Still widely cited as a reference point for code hygiene and craft, even when teams disagree on specifics.

### [The Cathedral and the Bazaar](../cards/cathedral-and-the-bazaar.md)
*Eric S. Raymond · 1999 · Essay · Beginner*

An influential essay contrasting two development models — the cathedral (closed, top-down) and the bazaar (open, decentralized) — using Linux as the case study. It helped legitimize open-source development to industry.

**Why it matters:** Shaped the open-source movement and the way modern software is built and shipped.

### [The Clean Coder](../cards/the-clean-coder.md)
*Robert C. Martin · 2011 · Book · Intermediate*

A code-of-conduct style book about professionalism, estimation, testing, and responsibility.

**Why it matters:** Useful for junior-to-mid engineers learning the social side of software craft.

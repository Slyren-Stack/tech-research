# AI Foundations

How AI actually works — the ideas, papers, and explainers that built modern machine learning and the transformer era.

18 item(s), ranked by signal. ⭐ = core / must-experience.

### ⭐ [Neural Networks: Zero to Hero](../cards/karpathy-zero-to-hero.md)
*Andrej Karpathy · 2023 · Course (Video) · Advanced*

A free, code-first lecture series building neural networks and a GPT from scratch in Python, including the famous 'Let's build GPT' walkthrough. You write every line and understand every step.

**Why it matters:** The definitive hands-on path to genuinely understanding modern LLMs by building one yourself.

### ⭐ [CS224N: Natural Language Processing with Deep Learning](../cards/cs224n-natural-language-processing-with-deep-learning.md)
*Stanford University · 2023 · Course · Advanced*

A thorough introduction to deep learning for NLP, including the fundamentals behind modern large language models.

**Why it matters:** One of the cleanest public paths from NLP basics to the architecture and training ideas behind LLMs.

### ⭐ [Attention Is All You Need](../cards/attention-is-all-you-need.md)
*Ashish Vaswani et al. (Google) · 2017 · Paper · Advanced*

The paper that introduced the Transformer, an architecture built entirely on attention mechanisms with no recurrence or convolution. It became the foundation for virtually every modern large language model.

**Why it matters:** Almost everything in the current AI wave traces back to this architecture. Reading it is the single best way to understand what an LLM actually is.

### ⭐ [Neural Networks (video series)](../cards/3blue1brown-neural-networks.md)
*Grant Sanderson (3Blue1Brown) · 2017 · Video Series · Beginner*

A beautifully animated video series that builds visual intuition for how neural networks learn, what gradient descent does, and how backpropagation works, all from first principles.

**Why it matters:** The single most effective way for a beginner to truly 'get' what a neural network is doing.

### ⭐ [The Bitter Lesson](../cards/the-bitter-lesson.md)
*Richard Sutton · 2019 · Essay · Beginner*

A short, influential essay arguing that across 70 years of AI research, general methods that leverage computation (search and learning) consistently beat approaches that build in human knowledge. The 'bitter' part is how often researchers resist this.

**Why it matters:** A two-page mental model that explains the trajectory of modern AI and why scaling worked.

### [CS231n: Deep Learning for Computer Vision](../cards/cs231n-deep-learning-for-computer-vision.md)
*Stanford University · 2021 · Course · Advanced*

A deep dive into end-to-end deep learning for visual recognition, including CNNs, training/debugging, and modern vision architectures.

**Why it matters:** A classic practical ML course that explains how deep vision systems are actually built and why they work.

### [Deep Residual Learning for Image Recognition](../cards/deep-residual-learning-for-image-recognition.md)
*He, Zhang, Ren, Sun · 2015 · Paper · Advanced*

Introduces residual learning to make very deep neural networks easier to optimize and more accurate.

**Why it matters:** A foundational paper behind the practical success of deep neural nets across vision and beyond.

### [Training Compute-Optimal Large Language Models](../cards/training-compute-optimal-large-language-models.md)
*Hoffmann et al. (DeepMind) · 2022 · Paper · Advanced*

Shows that many large language models are undertrained and derives compute-optimal scaling rules for model size and token count.

**Why it matters:** Critical for understanding why modern foundation models are trained the way they are.

### ⭐ [Computing Machinery and Intelligence](../cards/computing-machinery-and-intelligence.md)
*Alan Turing · 1950 · Essay · Intermediate*

Turing's seminal essay asking 'Can machines think?' and proposing the Imitation Game (the Turing Test) as an operational substitute for the question. It anticipated and rebutted most objections to machine intelligence still debated today.

**Why it matters:** The intellectual starting point of the entire field of AI, written before the first AI program existed.

### [Deep Learning (Nature review)](../cards/deep-learning-nature-2015.md)
*Yann LeCun, Yoshua Bengio, Geoffrey Hinton · 2015 · Paper · Intermediate*

An authoritative review of deep learning by three of its principal architects (later Turing Award winners), covering representation learning, backpropagation, CNNs, and RNNs in accessible form.

**Why it matters:** The best single-paper orientation to deep learning, written for a general scientific audience.

### [ImageNet Classification with Deep Convolutional Neural Networks (AlexNet)](../cards/alexnet-imagenet-2012.md)
*Alex Krizhevsky, Ilya Sutskever, Geoffrey Hinton · 2012 · Paper · Advanced*

The paper behind AlexNet, the deep convolutional network that crushed the ImageNet 2012 competition and kicked off the modern deep learning era by showing GPUs + big data + deep nets work at scale.

**Why it matters:** The 'big bang' moment of the deep learning revolution — the result that made industry take neural nets seriously.

### [Machine Learning (Stanford CS229 / Coursera)](../cards/andrew-ng-machine-learning.md)
*Andrew Ng · 2011 · Course · Intermediate*

The course that introduced millions of people to machine learning, covering supervised and unsupervised learning, regularization, and practical advice for applying ML. Famous for clear pedagogy over hype.

**Why it matters:** The canonical first ML course; shaped how a generation of practitioners thinks about the field.

### [AI History (Stanford CS221: Artificial Intelligence)](../cards/cs221-ai-history.md)
*Stanford CS221 / Stanford Online · 2021 · Lecture (Video) · Intermediate*

A lecture segment from Stanford's CS221 (Artificial Intelligence: Principles and Techniques, Autumn 2021) tracing the history of AI — from early symbolic systems and expert systems through the rise of statistical learning and modern deep learning.

**Why it matters:** Historical context from a rigorous university AI course helps builders understand why the field looks the way it does today.

### [On the Dangers of Stochastic Parrots](../cards/on-the-dangers-of-stochastic-parrots.md)
*Bender, Gebru, McMillan-Major, Shmitchell · 2021 · Paper · Advanced*

An influential critique arguing large language models are 'stochastic parrots' that risk scale-driven harms, bias, and environmental cost without true understanding.

**Why it matters:** The sharpest counterweight to scale-maximalism, and a story about power inside Big Tech research.

### [Sparks of Artificial General Intelligence: Early experiments with GPT-4](../cards/sparks-of-artificial-general-intelligence-early-experiments.md)
*Bubeck et al. (Microsoft Research) · 2023 · Paper · Advanced*

A Microsoft Research report claiming GPT-4 shows early sparks of general intelligence across diverse tasks.

**Why it matters:** It crystallized the 'is this AGI?' argument with concrete examples people still cite.

### [AI & Machine Learning (Stanford CS106E Lecture Notes)](../cards/cs106e-ai-machine-learning-notes.md)
*Stanford CS106E · Unknown · Lecture Notes (PDF) · Beginner*

Lecture notes from Stanford's CS106E (a computing course for general audiences) introducing artificial intelligence and machine learning concepts in plain, non-specialist language.

**Why it matters:** One of the most accessible institution-backed introductions to what AI and ML actually are.

### [Introduction to Graduate Artificial Intelligence (CMU 15-780)](../cards/cmu-15780-intro-ai.md)

Introductory lecture slides from Carnegie Mellon's graduate AI course (15-780), framing the scope of AI: search, logic, probabilistic reasoning, and machine learning at a graduate level.

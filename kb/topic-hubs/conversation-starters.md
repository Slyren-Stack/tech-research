# Conversation Starters

High-signal topics worth raising in technical and founder circles — each is something smart people genuinely argue about. A cross-cutting collection; every item also lives in its home hub.

**73 conversation-worthy items** across every theme, ranked by conversation value. Each also lives in its home hub.

> **The recurring themes:** Silicon Valley conversation keeps circling a handful of durable tensions: does scale beat cleverness (AI), do founders deserve special operating freedom, how do distribution and network effects manufacture moats, what does software's history teach about its future, and where do markets, regulation, and human attention push back on all of it.

### Startup History (13)
- **[Zero to One](../cards/zero-to-one.md)** — *Peter Thiel with Blake Masters, 2014 (Book)* — 'Competition is for losers' and the monopoly thesis are perennial debate-starters.
- **[Aggregation Theory](../cards/aggregation-theory.md)** — *Ben Thompson (Stratechery), 2015 (Essay)* — 'Aggregator vs. platform' is core Valley vocabulary — wield it and you sound fluent.
- **[Why Software Is Eating the World](../cards/why-software-is-eating-the-world.md)** — *Marc Andreessen, 2011 (Essay)* — The phrase is industry shorthand; everyone can riff on what software ate next.
- **[The Innovator's Dilemma](../cards/the-innovator-s-dilemma.md)** — *Clayton M. Christensen, 1997 (Book)* — Everyone invokes 'disruption' — few use it correctly, which is exactly why it sparks debate.
- **[The Hard Thing About Hard Things](../cards/hard-thing-about-hard-things.md)** — *Ben Horowitz, 2014 (Book)* — The honest-about-the-pain founder book everyone has a take on.
- **[Amazon Shareholder Letters](../cards/amazon-shareholder-letters.md)** — *Jeff Bezos, 1997 (Letters)* — Citing 'Day 1' or a Bezos letter signals you study operating excellence.
- **[7 Powers: The Foundations of Business Strategy](../cards/7-powers-the-foundations-of-business-strategy.md)** — *Hamilton Helmer, 2016 (Book)* — Naming a startup's specific 'power' is a sharp, status-y way to analyze moats.
- **[The Lean Startup](../cards/the-lean-startup.md)** — *Eric Ries, 2011 (Book)* — Loved and backlashed against — MVPs and pivots still start arguments.
- **[The Mom Test](../cards/the-mom-test.md)** — *Rob Fitzpatrick, 2013 (Book)* — Everyone has a 'customers lied to me' story this book frames perfectly.
- **[Crossing the Chasm](../cards/crossing-the-chasm.md)** — *Geoffrey A. Moore, 2014 (Book)* — The default frame for arguing why a hot product will (or won't) reach the mainstream.
- **[Only the Paranoid Survive](../cards/only-the-paranoid-survive.md)** — *Andy Grove, 1996 (Book)* — Frame AI as the current 'strategic inflection point' and you've started a strategy debate.
- **[The Cold Start Problem: How to Start and Scale Network Effects](../cards/cold-start-problem-andrew-chen.md)** — *Andrew Chen, Unknown (Talk)* — The shared vocabulary for arguing about network effects and 'atomic networks.'
- **[Super Founders](../cards/super-founders.md)** — *Ali Tamaseb, 2021 (Book)* — Drop a counterintuitive stat and watch investors defend their pattern-matching.

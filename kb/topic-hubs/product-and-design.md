### ⭐ [Continuous Discovery Habits](../cards/continuous-discovery-habits.md)
*Teresa Torres · 2021 · Book · Intermediate*

A system for weekly customer touchpoints, small research activities, and evidence-based product decisions.

**Why it matters:** The clearest public framework for making product discovery continuous rather than occasional.

### ⭐ [The Design of Everyday Things](../cards/design-of-everyday-things.md)
*Don Norman · 1988 · Book · Beginner*

The foundational text of user-centered design, introducing affordances, signifiers, mapping, and feedback. It teaches you to blame bad design, not users, and to see the designed world everywhere.

**Why it matters:** The book that defined human-centered design and gave the field its shared language.

### [Tim Brown - From Design to Design Thinking](../cards/tim-brown-from-design-to-design-thinking.md)
*Tim Brown · 2009 · Talk · Beginner*

A classic introduction to design thinking as a human-centered approach that links desirability, viability, and feasibility.

**Why it matters:** It helped define the language of modern product/design collaboration.

### [The three ways that good design makes you happy](../cards/the-three-ways-that-good-design-makes-you-happy.md)
*Don Norman · 2003 · Talk · Beginner*

A foundational talk on emotional design, showing why useful products still fail if they are not emotionally intelligible or pleasurable.

**Why it matters:** It remains a concise bridge between psychology and product craft.

### ⭐ [Inventing on Principle](../cards/inventing-on-principle.md)
*Bret Victor · 2012 · Talk · Beginner*

A landmark talk demonstrating live, immediate feedback between creators and their creations, and arguing that the most meaningful work comes from following a guiding principle. Visually stunning and intellectually provocative.

**Why it matters:** An endlessly influential talk on tools, creativity, and doing work that means something.

### [Marty Cagan on the Current 'Golden Era' for Product Management](../cards/marty-cagan-on-the-current-golden-era-for-product-management.md)

### [Shape Up](../cards/shape-up.md)
*37signals / Basecamp · 2019 · Book · Intermediate*

A distinctive product development method built around shaping, betting, appetite, and fixed timeboxes.

**Why it matters:** A useful counter-model for teams that are stuck in bloated roadmaps and endless backlog management.

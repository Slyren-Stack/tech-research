# Founder Mental Models

Durable ways of thinking about work, leverage, judgment, and doing great work over a career.

12 item(s), ranked by signal. ⭐ = core / must-experience.

---

### [Before the Startup](../cards/before-the-startup.md)
*Paul Graham · 2014 · Recorded lecture · Beginner*

A founder-centric lecture on how to think before you start: how ideas emerge, how founders get misled, and how to choose problems.

**Why it matters:** Paul Graham's influence on startup thinking is foundational, and this lecture shows the pre-company mental model YC pushes.

### ⭐ [Do Things That Don't Scale](../cards/do-things-that-dont-scale.md)
*Paul Graham · 2013 · Essay · Beginner*

A Paul Graham essay arguing that early-stage founders should do manual, unscalable things — personally recruiting users, delivering delight by hand — because that's how startups actually get their first traction.

**Why it matters:** Corrects the most common early-founder mistake: waiting to scale before earning traction.

### ⭐ [How to Do Great Work](../cards/how-to-do-great-work.md)
*Paul Graham · 2023 · Essay · Beginner*

A long essay distilling what people who do great work tend to have in common: working on things you're genuinely curious about, the discipline to follow that curiosity, and the courage to work on ambitious problems.

**Why it matters:** A modern, comprehensive guide to building an ambitious, meaningful career in any field.

### ⭐ [You and Your Research](../cards/hamming-you-and-your-research.md)
*Richard Hamming · 1986 · Talk · Intermediate*

A famous Bell Labs talk on why some scientists do Nobel-caliber work and others don't. Hamming argues it's less about raw genius and more about courage, working on important problems, and managing yourself.

**Why it matters:** A timeless meditation on how to aim a career at work that actually matters.

### [Essays by Paul Graham](../cards/essays-by-paul-graham.md)
*Paul Graham / Y Combinator · Unknown · Essays · Intermediate*

A deep essay corpus on startup ideas, hiring, growth, fundraising, and founder psychology.

**Why it matters:** Still one of the sharpest public collections of startup thinking ever published.

### [Maker's Schedule, Manager's Schedule](../cards/makers-schedule-managers-schedule.md)
*Paul Graham · 2009 · Essay · Beginner*

A short essay distinguishing the manager's schedule (chopped into hour-long meetings) from the maker's schedule (long uninterrupted blocks), and why meetings are so costly for people who build.

**Why it matters:** A small idea that reshapes how builders and their managers should structure time.

### [Poor Charlie's Almanack](../cards/poor-charlies-almanack.md)
*Charlie Munger (ed. Peter Kaufman) · 2005 · Book · Intermediate*

A collection of Charlie Munger's speeches and wisdom, centered on the idea of a 'latticework of mental models' drawn from many disciplines to make better decisions and avoid predictable folly.

**Why it matters:** The foundational text for the 'mental models' approach to thinking and decision-making.

### [How to Get Startup Ideas](../cards/how-to-get-startup-ideas.md)
*Paul Graham · 2024 · Video reading / commentary · Beginner*

A video reading of one of Paul Graham's most quoted startup essays: the best ideas are usually found, not invented.

**Why it matters:** Useful as a compact reinforcement of YC's idea-selection philosophy, even if it is not the original lecture.

### [How to Get Rich (without getting lucky)](../cards/naval-how-to-get-rich.md)
*Naval Ravikant · 2019 · Podcast Series · Beginner*

An expanded podcast/essay series on building wealth through ownership and leverage — code, media, capital, and labor — plus the role of specific knowledge, accountability, and long-term games with long-term people.

**Why it matters:** A widely shared mental-model framework for leverage, ownership, and judgment.

### [How Will You Measure Your Life?](../cards/how-will-you-measure-your-life.md)
*Clayton M. Christensen, James Allworth, Karen Dillon · 2012 · Book · Beginner*

A leadership and life framework that applies strategy thinking to personal and organizational choices.

**Why it matters:** Useful as a grounding text for founders who are also thinking about character, meaning, and company culture.

### [What You Do Is Who You Are](../cards/what-you-do-is-who-you-are.md)
*Ben Horowitz · 2019 · Book · Intermediate*

A book on company culture using history, examples, and practical advice for executives.

**Why it matters:** Helpful for leaders designing the norms and habits of a venture studio or startup team.

### [Stanford Commencement Address (2005)](../cards/steve-jobs-stanford-2005.md)
*Steve Jobs · 2005 · Talk · Beginner*

Steve Jobs's widely watched commencement speech told through three stories — connecting the dots, love and loss, and death — distilling a philosophy of following curiosity and doing what you love.

**Why it matters:** A short, durable piece of motivation for builders facing uncertainty.

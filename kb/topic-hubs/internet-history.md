# Internet and Big Tech History

Where computing and the internet came from — the people, labs, and machines that made it all possible.

20 item(s), ranked by signal. ⭐ = core / must-experience.

---

### [Steve Jobs Introduces the Macintosh](../cards/steve-jobs-introduces-the-macintosh.md)
*Steve Jobs · 1984 · Historical keynote · Beginner*

The famous Macintosh unveiling, a defining product launch that shows how narrative, reveal, and product demo can become culture.

**Why it matters:** It is one of the most important product launch recordings ever made.

### [Steve Jobs: The Lost Interview](../cards/steve-jobs-the-lost-interview.md)
*Steve Jobs, Robert X. Cringely · 1995 · Interview documentary · Beginner*

A candid long-form interview with Jobs between Apple eras, notable for his thinking on products, Xerox PARC, and Microsoft.

**Why it matters:** It offers one of the clearest windows into Jobs' product mind outside of stage demos.

### [Computer History Museum Oral Histories](../cards/computer-history-museum-oral-histories.md)
*Computer History Museum · Unknown · Archive · Intermediate*

A large archive of firsthand interviews and demonstrations with pioneers in computing, entrepreneurship, and networking.

**Why it matters:** One of the richest primary-source archives for tech history and industry memory.

### ⭐ [The Mother of All Demos](../cards/mother-of-all-demos.md)
*Douglas Engelbart · 1968 · Video · Beginner*

Engelbart's 1968 live demonstration that introduced the mouse, hypertext, real-time collaborative editing, video conferencing, and windows — decades before they became mainstream. A staggering vision of interactive computing.

**Why it matters:** The single most prophetic demo in computing history — the future, shown decades early.

### [As We May Think](../cards/as-we-may-think.md)
*Vannevar Bush · 1945 · Essay · Intermediate*

A 1945 Atlantic essay imagining the 'Memex', a device for storing and linking all of one's books, records, and communications by association — a striking anticipation of hypertext, personal computing, and the web.

**Why it matters:** The conceptual seed of hypertext and the web, written before computers were programmable as we know them.

### [Bad Blood: Secrets and Lies in a Silicon Valley Startup](../cards/bad-blood-secrets-and-lies-in-a-silicon-valley-startup.md)
*John Carreyrou · 2018 · Book · Beginner*

The definitive account of Theranos's rise and fraud, by the journalist who broke the story.

**Why it matters:** The ultimate cautionary tale about hype, governance, and the dark side of founder worship.

### [The Soul of a New Machine](../cards/soul-of-a-new-machine.md)
*Tracy Kidder · 1981 · Book · Beginner*

A Pulitzer-winning account of a team at Data General racing to build a new minicomputer, capturing the obsession, burnout, and craft of hardware engineering with novelistic detail.

**Why it matters:** The classic narrative of engineering teams under pressure; still the best book on the feeling of building.

### [The Everything Store: Jeff Bezos and the Age of Amazon](../cards/the-everything-store-jeff-bezos-and-the-age-of-amazon.md)
*Brad Stone · 2013 · Book · Beginner*

A deeply reported history of Amazon's rise and Bezos's relentless operating culture.

**Why it matters:** The reference history for Amazon's strategy and culture, endlessly cited in operating debates.

### [The Dream Machine](../cards/the-dream-machine.md)
*M. Mitchell Waldrop · 2001 · Book · Intermediate*

The story of J.C.R. Licklider and the visionaries who shaped personal and networked computing.

**Why it matters:** A beloved deep history that signals real literacy in where computing came from.

### [Amazon's Antitrust Paradox](../cards/amazon-s-antitrust-paradox.md)
*Lina Khan · 2017 · Law journal article · Advanced*

The Yale Law Journal note arguing consumer-welfare antitrust fails to capture Amazon's structural power.

**Why it matters:** It moved tech antitrust from academic to live policy — its author became FTC chair.

### [Hackers: Heroes of the Computer Revolution](../cards/hackers-heroes-levy.md)
*Steven Levy · 1984 · Book · Beginner*

A history of the hacker culture from MIT's early labs through the home-computer and game eras, documenting the ethos — openness, hands-on imperative, mistrust of authority — that shaped tech.

**Why it matters:** The canonical chronicle of where hacker and builder culture came from.

### [The Innovators](../cards/the-innovators-isaacson.md)
*Walter Isaacson · 2014 · Book · Beginner*

A sweeping history of the digital revolution from Ada Lovelace to the web, emphasizing that innovation is collaborative — a relay of teams and ideas rather than lone geniuses.

**Why it matters:** A single readable narrative tying together the whole arc of computing history.

### [The Age of Surveillance Capitalism](../cards/the-age-of-surveillance-capitalism.md)
*Shoshana Zuboff · 2019 · Book · Advanced*

An argument that tech giants commodify behavioral data into 'behavioral futures' markets.

**Why it matters:** The most-cited intellectual critique of the ad-driven internet's business model.

### [The Attention Merchants](../cards/the-attention-merchants.md)
*Tim Wu · 2016 · Book · Intermediate*

A history of how industries from newspapers to social media have harvested and resold human attention.

**Why it matters:** Gives historical depth to the attention-economy conversation that dominates tech.

### [Where Wizards Stay Up Late: The Origins of the Internet](../cards/where-wizards-stay-up-late.md)
*Katie Hafner, Matthew Lyon · 1996 · Book · Beginner*

The story of the people who built ARPANET, the precursor to the internet — the researchers, the funding, and the engineering decisions (like packet switching) that made a global network possible.

**Why it matters:** The definitive popular history of how the internet was born.

### [Valley of Genius](../cards/valley-of-genius.md)
*Adam Fisher · 2018 · Book · Beginner*

An oral history of Silicon Valley told through the voices of the people who built it.

**Why it matters:** A lively, quotable source of Valley lore and origin stories.

### [Nexus: A Brief History of Information Networks](../cards/nexus-a-brief-history-of-information-networks.md)
*Yuval Noah Harari · 2024 · Book · Beginner*

A sweeping history of information networks from myth and writing to AI, arguing that how societies handle information determines their fate.

**Why it matters:** A mainstream big-think frame that puts AI in deep historical context — heavily referenced.

### [The Californian Ideology](../cards/the-californian-ideology.md)
*Richard Barbrook & Andy Cameron · 1995 · Essay · Advanced*

A prescient critique of Silicon Valley's fusion of countercultural libertarianism and free-market tech optimism.

**Why it matters:** It named the Valley's worldview decades early; still cited to explain tech politics.

### [Snow Crash](../cards/snow-crash.md)
*Neal Stephenson · 1992 · Book · Intermediate*

The cyberpunk novel that coined the 'Metaverse' and shaped how technologists imagine virtual worlds.

**Why it matters:** Required cultural reading behind metaverse and VR ambitions; a shared imaginative reference.

### [Chaos Monkeys](../cards/chaos-monkeys.md)
*Antonio García Martínez · 2016 · Book · Beginner*

A caustic, insider memoir of startup life, YC, and ad-tech inside Facebook.

**Why it matters:** A polarizing insider account that people love to argue about for its candor and controversy.

# Conversation Topics

A high-signal conversation library for Silicon Valley circles.

## AI and Frontier Tech
- **The Bitter Lesson** (2019) — Rich Sutton
  - A short, famous argument that general methods using computation tend to beat hand-built knowledge over time.
  - Why it is a good conversation topic: It instantly opens the scale-versus-craft debate that dominates AI rooms.
  - Audience: AI researchers, founders, engineers, investors
  - Difficulty: advanced | Conversation value: 10/10 | Credibility: 10/10
  - Link: https://www.incompleteideas.net/IncIdeas/BitterLesson.html
- **Attention Is All You Need** (2017) — Ashish Vaswani et al.
  - The paper that introduced the Transformer architecture and made modern LLMs possible.
  - Why it is a good conversation topic: It is the technical before-and-after line for the generative AI era.
  - Audience: Engineers, AI founders, researchers
  - Difficulty: advanced | Conversation value: 10/10 | Credibility: 10/10
  - Link: https://arxiv.org/abs/1706.03762
- **GPT-4 Technical Report** (2023) — OpenAI
  - OpenAI’s flagship disclosure on GPT-4’s multimodality, benchmark performance, and limitations.
  - Why it is a good conversation topic: It bridges model capability, product strategy, and the practical reality of frontier AI.
  - Audience: Founders, AI engineers, product teams, investors
  - Difficulty: intermediate | Conversation value: 10/10 | Credibility: 10/10
  - Link: https://cdn.openai.com/papers/gpt-4.pdf
- **Software Is Changing (Again)** (2025) — Andrej Karpathy / Y Combinator
  - Karpathy reframes software as shifting again under AI, with English, prompts, and agents becoming part of the programming stack.
  - Why it is a good conversation topic: It is one of the clearest ways to discuss AI-native software without falling into hype.
  - Audience: Founders, engineers, product people
  - Difficulty: intermediate | Conversation value: 10/10 | Credibility: 10/10
  - Link: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LCEmiRjPEtQ
- **2026 AI Index Report** (2026) — Stanford HAI
  - The most current Stanford AI Index reports AI investment, capability progress, adoption, and governance gaps.
  - Why it is a good conversation topic: It gives people a shared factual baseline instead of vibes.
  - Audience: Founders, investors, researchers, operators
  - Difficulty: intermediate | Conversation value: 9/10 | Credibility: 10/10
  - Link: https://hai.stanford.edu/ai-index/2026-ai-index-report

## Startups and Founder Culture
- **Startup = Growth** (2012) — Paul Graham
  - One of Graham’s clearest statements that small differences in startup growth rate compound into radically different outcomes.
  - Why it is a good conversation topic: Founders and investors constantly argue about growth quality, and this essay gives them a shared language.
  - Audience: Founders, investors, operators
  - Difficulty: beginner | Conversation value: 10/10 | Credibility: 10/10
  - Link: https://www.paulgraham.com/growth.html
- **Do Things that Don’t Scale** (2013) — Paul Graham
  - A canonical YC-era argument for doing manual, unscalable work to get the first users and learn what matters.
  - Why it is a good conversation topic: Almost every founder can tell a 'we did the ugly thing early' story.
  - Audience: Founders, PMs, growth teams
  - Difficulty: beginner | Conversation value: 10/10 | Credibility: 10/10
  - Link: https://www.paulgraham.com/ds.html
- **Before the Startup** (2014) — Paul Graham
  - Talks about how founders think before they start, how ideas emerge, and why judgment matters more than planning.
  - Why it is a good conversation topic: A natural entry point for anyone deciding whether to start a company.
  - Audience: Pre-founders, founders, students, investors
  - Difficulty: beginner | Conversation value: 9/10 | Credibility: 10/10
  - Link: https://www.paulgraham.com/before.html
- **Founder Mode** (2024) — Paul Graham
  - Argues that founders should sometimes manage companies differently from professional managers as they scale.
  - Why it is a good conversation topic: It touches a live Silicon Valley argument about founder autonomy, management, and culture.
  - Audience: Founders, operators, investors
  - Difficulty: intermediate | Conversation value: 10/10 | Credibility: 9/10
  - Link: https://www.paulgraham.com/foundermode.html
- **How to Get Startup Ideas** (2012) — Paul Graham
  - Says good startup ideas are usually found by observing real problems rather than brainstorming them abstractly.
  - Why it is a good conversation topic: Idea quality and problem selection are evergreen founder-room arguments.
  - Audience: Founders, investors, operators
  - Difficulty: beginner | Conversation value: 9/10 | Credibility: 10/10
  - Link: https://www.paulgraham.com/startupideas.html

## Concise synthesis
Silicon Valley conversation keeps circling a few durable questions: does scale beat theory, do founders need special operating freedom, how do network effects and distribution create moats, and how do funds, LPs, and regulation shape what gets built?

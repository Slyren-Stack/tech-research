# Venture Capital History Intelligence Archive

A curated, ranked archive of books, articles, talks, documentaries, interviews, panels, and oral histories on the history of venture capital.

## What this archive covers
Origins of venture capital, Sand Hill Road and Silicon Valley, LP/GP fundraising, angel investing and accelerators, corporate VC, secondaries and continuation funds, booms and busts, critiques, and lesser-known historical material.

## Core Historical Foundations

**1. VC: An American History** — Tom Nicholas / Harvard University Press (2019)  
The strongest single-volume history of venture capital in the United States, from early risk finance to modern Silicon Valley.  
*Key themes:* risk pooling, institutional evolution, innovation finance  
*Why it matters:* It gives the broadest and most durable framework for understanding how VC became an American institution.  
*Audience:* researcher, investor, founder, operator | *Difficulty:* intermediate | *Credibility:* 10/10 | *Usefulness:* 10/10  
*Link:* https://www.hup.harvard.edu/books/9780674988002  

**2. Creative Capital: Georges Doriot and the Birth of Venture Capital** — Spencer E. Ante / Harvard Business Press (2008)  
The classic account of Georges Doriot and ARD, often treated as the birth of modern venture capital.  
*Key themes:* ARD, Doriot, institutional invention, early tech finance  
*Why it matters:* It explains how modern venture capital emerged as a distinct profession and structure.  
*Audience:* researcher, investor, founder | *Difficulty:* intermediate | *Credibility:* 9/10 | *Usefulness:* 9/10  
*Link:* https://books.google.com/books/about/Creative_Capital.html?id=Pex8b-qNoL8C  

**3. The Money of Invention: How Venture Capital Creates New Wealth** — Josh Lerner and Paul Gompers / Harvard Business School Press (2001)  
A classic explanation of how VC works structurally and why it became a durable engine of new company formation.  
*Key themes:* startup finance, VC economics, wealth creation, exit logic  
*Why it matters:* It remains one of the cleanest bridges between history and practice.  
*Audience:* founder, investor, operator, researcher | *Difficulty:* beginner | *Credibility:* 10/10 | *Usefulness:* 10/10  
*Link:* https://www.hbs.edu/faculty/Pages/item.aspx?num=10434  

**4. The Power Law: Venture Capital and the Making of the New Future** — Sebastian Mallaby / Penguin Press (2022)  
A modern narrative history of the dominant firms, personalities, and strategy shifts that shaped the VC industry.  
*Key themes:* power-law returns, firm culture, Silicon Valley, winner-take-most dynamics  
*Why it matters:* Probably the best contemporary narrative of the modern VC industry.  
*Audience:* researcher, investor, founder | *Difficulty:* intermediate | *Credibility:* 10/10 | *Usefulness:* 10/10  
*Link:* https://www.cfr.org/books/power-law  

**5. How Venture Capital Works** — Harvard Business Review (1998)  
A concise evergreen explanation of the venture bargain: invest, nurture, and exit through sale or IPO.  
*Key themes:* equity financing, exit discipline, fund economics  
*Why it matters:* A clean structural primer for anyone trying to understand the industry.  
*Audience:* founder, operator, beginner investor | *Difficulty:* beginner | *Credibility:* 9/10 | *Usefulness:* 9/10  
*Link:* https://hbr.org/1998/11/how-venture-capital-works  

**6. How Do Venture Capitalists Make Decisions?** — Paul Gompers et al. / NBER (2016)  
A rare empirical look inside the black box of VC decision-making using a large survey of firms and partners.  
*Key themes:* screening, stage, sector choice, investment process  
*Why it matters:* It reveals how real firms behave rather than how they advertise themselves.  
*Audience:* researcher, investor, advanced founder | *Difficulty:* advanced | *Credibility:* 10/10 | *Usefulness:* 9/10  
*Link:* https://www.nber.org/system/files/working_papers/w22587/w22587.pdf  

**7. Venture Capital Data: Opportunities and Challenges** — Steven N. Kaplan and Josh Lerner / Harvard Business School (2016)  
A foundational paper on why VC is hard to study and how data limitations shape what we know about fund performance and LP behavior.  
*Key themes:* LP data, fund performance, measurement limits, private markets  
*Why it matters:* A necessary reality check for VC narratives built on incomplete public data.  
*Audience:* researcher, investor, policymaker | *Difficulty:* advanced | *Credibility:* 10/10 | *Usefulness:* 9/10  
*Link:* https://www.hbs.edu/ris/Publication%20Files/17-012_10de1f93-30e4-4a98-858c-4137556ec037.pdf  

**8. A survey of venture capital research** — Marco Da Rin, Thomas Hellmann, Manju Puri / NBER (2011)

## Concise synthesis

Venture capital emerges as an institutional solution to financing highly uncertain innovation, then becomes a concentrated and culturally powerful engine for tech formation. Its history is a mix of private initiative, public policy, founder networks, LP capital, and repeated boom-bust cycles.

## Gaps needing more research

Venture debt, startup bank financing, SaaS-era fundraising history, later-stage crossover funds, global VC outside the U.S., defense/climate/biotech VC sub-histories, governance and DEI, founder-friendly secondary mechanics, and the history of data rooms, term sheets, and startup legal infrastructure.

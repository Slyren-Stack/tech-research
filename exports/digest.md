## ⭐ Attention Is All You Need
*Ashish Vaswani et al. (Google) · 2017 · Paper · AI Foundations → Deep Learning Architectures*
`Advanced` · cred 10/10 · useful 9/10 · status `core`

The paper that introduced the Transformer, an architecture built entirely on attention mechanisms with no recurrence or convolution. It became the foundation for virtually every modern large language model.

**Key takeaways:** (1) Self-attention lets a model weigh relationships between all tokens in a sequence in parallel. (2) Removing recurrence made training dramatically more parallelizable and scalable. (3) The architecture generalized far beyond translation into the backbone of GPT, BERT, and modern LLMs.

**Why it matters:** Almost everything in the current AI wave traces back to this architecture. Reading it is the single best way to understand what an LLM actually is.

**Source:** [https://arxiv.org/abs/1706.03762](https://arxiv.org/abs/1706.03762)

## ⭐ The Bitter Lesson
*Richard Sutton · 2019 · Essay · AI Foundations → Research Philosophy*
`Beginner` · cred 9/10 · useful 9/10 · status `core`

A short, influential essay arguing that across 70 years of AI research, general methods that leverage computation (search and learning) consistently beat approaches that build in human knowledge. The 'bitter' part is how often researchers resist this.

**Key takeaways:** (1) General methods that scale with compute beat hand-crafted human knowledge over time. (2) Building in our own understanding feels good short-term but caps long-term progress. (3) Bet on learning and search, not on encoding domain expertise.

**Why it matters:** A two-page mental model that explains the trajectory of modern AI and why scaling worked.

**Source:** [http://www.incompleteideas.net/IncIdeas/BitterLesson.html](http://www.incompleteideas.net/IncIdeas/BitterLesson.html)

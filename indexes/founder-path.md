# Founder / Startup Path

**For:** People starting or operating a company who want judgment, not platitudes.

The working founder's canon: company-building, fundraising, product, and the mental models that hold up under pressure.

Work through these in order:

1. **[How to Start a Startup (Stanford CS183B)](../kb/cards/how-to-start-a-startup-cs183b.md)** — Sam Altman, Y Combinator, et al., 2014 (Lecture Series, Beginner)  
   The most comprehensive free startup curriculum, taught by the people who define the field.
2. **[The Hard Thing About Hard Things](../kb/cards/hard-thing-about-hard-things.md)** — Ben Horowitz, 2014 (Book, Intermediate)  
   The definitive read on the operational and emotional reality of leading a startup.
3. **[Zero to One](../kb/cards/zero-to-one.md)** — Peter Thiel with Blake Masters, 2014 (Book, Beginner)  
   A widely read framework for thinking about differentiation, monopoly, and ambition.
4. **[Founders at Work](../kb/cards/founders-at-work.md)** — Jessica Livingston, 2007 (Book, Beginner)  
   A grounding antidote to startup mythology, from a Y Combinator co-founder.
5. **[Do Things That Don't Scale](../kb/cards/do-things-that-dont-scale.md)** — Paul Graham, 2013 (Essay, Beginner)  
   Corrects the most common early-founder mistake: waiting to scale before earning traction.
6. **[Inspired: How to Create Tech Products Customers Love](../kb/cards/inspired-cagan.md)** — Marty Cagan, 2017 (Book, Intermediate)  
   The default reference for modern product management practice.
7. **[Secrets of Sand Hill Road](../kb/cards/secrets-of-sand-hill-road.md)** — Scott Kupor, 2019 (Book, Intermediate)  
   The most accessible explanation of the VC machine from the inside.
8. **[How to Raise Money](../kb/cards/pg-how-to-raise-money.md)** — Paul Graham, 2013 (Essay, Intermediate)  
   One of the clearest practical guides to seed fundraising from someone who's seen thousands of rounds.
9. **[Venture Deals](../kb/cards/venture-deals.md)** — Brad Feld, Jason Mendelson, 2011 (Book, Intermediate)  
   The go-to handbook for founders who want to understand deals before signing them.
10. **[How to Get Rich (without getting lucky)](../kb/cards/naval-how-to-get-rich.md)** — Naval Ravikant, 2019 (Podcast Series, Beginner)  
   A widely shared mental-model framework for leverage, ownership, and judgment.
11. **[You and Your Research](../kb/cards/hamming-you-and-your-research.md)** — Richard Hamming, 1986 (Talk, Intermediate)  
   A timeless meditation on how to aim a career at work that actually matters.

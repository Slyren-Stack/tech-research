# Beginner Path

**For:** Newcomers to tech, AI, and startups who want a clear, motivating on-ramp.

Start with intuition and story before depth. These build a mental map of how AI works, where startups come from, and what good design feels like — without assuming a technical background.

Work through these in order:

1. **[Neural Networks (video series)](../kb/cards/3blue1brown-neural-networks.md)** — Grant Sanderson (3Blue1Brown), 2017 (Video Series, Beginner)  
   The single most effective way for a beginner to truly 'get' what a neural network is doing.
2. **[As We May Think](../kb/cards/as-we-may-think.md)** — Vannevar Bush, 1945 (Essay, Intermediate)  
   The conceptual seed of hypertext and the web, written before computers were programmable as we know them.
3. **[The Mother of All Demos](../kb/cards/mother-of-all-demos.md)** — Douglas Engelbart, 1968 (Video, Beginner)  
   The single most prophetic demo in computing history — the future, shown decades early.
4. **[The Design of Everyday Things](../kb/cards/design-of-everyday-things.md)** — Don Norman, 1988 (Book, Beginner)  
   The book that defined human-centered design and gave the field its shared language.
5. **[Do Things That Don't Scale](../kb/cards/do-things-that-dont-scale.md)** — Paul Graham, 2013 (Essay, Beginner)  
   Corrects the most common early-founder mistake: waiting to scale before earning traction.
6. **[How to Do Great Work](../kb/cards/how-to-do-great-work.md)** — Paul Graham, 2023 (Essay, Beginner)  
   A modern, comprehensive guide to building an ambitious, meaningful career in any field.
7. **[Zero to One](../kb/cards/zero-to-one.md)** — Peter Thiel with Blake Masters, 2014 (Book, Beginner)  
   A widely read framework for thinking about differentiation, monopoly, and ambition.
8. **[General Magic](../kb/cards/general-magic-documentary.md)** — Sarah Kerruish, Matt Maude, 2018 (Documentary, Beginner)  
   A rare, honest film about a brilliant failure that quietly shaped modern tech.
9. **[Software 2.0](../kb/cards/software-2-0.md)** — Andrej Karpathy, 2017 (Essay, Intermediate)  
   A clarifying mental model for anyone building AI products — it changes what 'programming' means.
10. **[Stanford Commencement Address (2005)](../kb/cards/steve-jobs-stanford-2005.md)** — Steve Jobs, 2005 (Talk, Beginner)  
   A short, durable piece of motivation for builders facing uncertainty.

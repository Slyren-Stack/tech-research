# AI Engineering Path

**For:** Engineers and ML teams building with models, from fundamentals to frontier systems.

A technical progression from machine-learning foundations through the architectures and scaling laws behind modern LLMs, ending with applied, build-it-yourself and state-of-the-field material.

Work through these in order:

1. **[Machine Learning (Stanford CS229 / Coursera)](../kb/cards/andrew-ng-machine-learning.md)** — Andrew Ng, 2011 (Course, Intermediate)  
   The canonical first ML course; shaped how a generation of practitioners thinks about the field.
2. **[CS231n: Deep Learning for Computer Vision](../kb/cards/cs231n-deep-learning-for-computer-vision.md)** — Stanford University, 2021 (Course, Advanced)  
   A classic practical ML course that explains how deep vision systems are actually built and why they work.
3. **[CS224N: Natural Language Processing with Deep Learning](../kb/cards/cs224n-natural-language-processing-with-deep-learning.md)** — Stanford University, 2023 (Course, Advanced)  
   One of the cleanest public paths from NLP basics to the architecture and training ideas behind LLMs.
4. **[Attention Is All You Need](../kb/cards/attention-is-all-you-need.md)** — Ashish Vaswani et al. (Google), 2017 (Paper, Advanced)  
   Almost everything in the current AI wave traces back to this architecture. Reading it is the single best way to understand what an LLM actually is.
5. **[Deep Residual Learning for Image Recognition](../kb/cards/deep-residual-learning-for-image-recognition.md)** — He, Zhang, Ren, Sun, 2015 (Paper, Advanced)  
   A foundational paper behind the practical success of deep neural nets across vision and beyond.
6. **[Training Compute-Optimal Large Language Models](../kb/cards/training-compute-optimal-large-language-models.md)** — Hoffmann et al. (DeepMind), 2022 (Paper, Advanced)  
   Critical for understanding why modern foundation models are trained the way they are.
7. **[Neural Networks: Zero to Hero](../kb/cards/karpathy-zero-to-hero.md)** — Andrej Karpathy, 2023 (Course (Video), Advanced)  
   The definitive hands-on path to genuinely understanding modern LLMs by building one yourself.
8. **[GPT-4 Technical Report](../kb/cards/gpt-4-technical-report.md)** — OpenAI, 2023 (Technical report, Advanced)  
   A canonical public reference for what frontier model releases look like in practice.
9. **[Software Is Changing (Again)](../kb/cards/software-is-changing-again.md)** — Andrej Karpathy, 2025 (Talk, Intermediate)  
   This is one of the best current conceptual maps for AI-native product teams.
10. **[Artificial Intelligence Index Report 2024](../kb/cards/artificial-intelligence-index-report-2024.md)** — Stanford HAI / AI Index Steering Committee, 2024 (Report, Intermediate)  
   Best single macro reference for how the AI field is changing year to year.

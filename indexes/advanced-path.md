# Advanced Builder Path

**For:** Engineers and technical builders who want depth in AI and systems.

Primary sources and rigorous material. Expect papers, dense talks, and books that reward re-reading. Assumes comfort with code and basic math.

Work through these in order:

1. **[Attention Is All You Need](../kb/cards/attention-is-all-you-need.md)** — Ashish Vaswani et al. (Google), 2017 (Paper, Advanced)  
   Almost everything in the current AI wave traces back to this architecture. Reading it is the single best way to understand what an LLM actually is.
2. **[ImageNet Classification with Deep Convolutional Neural Networks (AlexNet)](../kb/cards/alexnet-imagenet-2012.md)** — Alex Krizhevsky, Ilya Sutskever, Geoffrey Hinton, 2012 (Paper, Advanced)  
   The 'big bang' moment of the deep learning revolution — the result that made industry take neural nets seriously.
3. **[Deep Learning (Nature review)](../kb/cards/deep-learning-nature-2015.md)** — Yann LeCun, Yoshua Bengio, Geoffrey Hinton, 2015 (Paper, Intermediate)  
   The best single-paper orientation to deep learning, written for a general scientific audience.
4. **[Neural Networks: Zero to Hero](../kb/cards/karpathy-zero-to-hero.md)** — Andrej Karpathy, 2023 (Course (Video), Advanced)  
   The definitive hands-on path to genuinely understanding modern LLMs by building one yourself.
5. **[Designing Data-Intensive Applications](../kb/cards/designing-data-intensive-applications.md)** — Martin Kleppmann, 2017 (Book, Advanced)  
   The single most recommended systems book for backend engineers in the last decade.
6. **[Time, Clocks, and the Ordering of Events in a Distributed System](../kb/cards/time-clocks-ordering-lamport.md)** — Leslie Lamport, 1978 (Paper, Advanced)  
   One of the most cited and important papers in computer science; the root of how we reason about distributed time.
7. **[End-to-End Arguments in System Design](../kb/cards/end-to-end-arguments.md)** — J. Saltzer, D. Reed, D. Clark, 1984 (Paper, Advanced)  
   One of the deepest design principles in computing, and a key reason the internet scaled.
8. **[Out of the Tar Pit](../kb/cards/out-of-the-tar-pit.md)** — Ben Moseley, Peter Marks, 2006 (Paper, Advanced)  
   A rigorous articulation of why simplicity is the core engineering virtue.
9. **[Structure and Interpretation of Computer Programs (SICP)](../kb/cards/sicp.md)** — Harold Abelson, Gerald Jay Sussman, Julie Sussman, 1985 (Book, Advanced)  
   Among the most influential CS texts ever written; a rite of passage for serious programmers.
10. **[The Bitter Lesson](../kb/cards/the-bitter-lesson.md)** — Richard Sutton, 2019 (Essay, Beginner)  
   A two-page mental model that explains the trajectory of modern AI and why scaling worked.
11. **[State of GPT](../kb/cards/state-of-gpt.md)** — Andrej Karpathy, 2023 (Talk, Intermediate)  
   One of the clearest explanations of how production LLMs are built and how to wield them.

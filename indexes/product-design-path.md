# Product & Design Path

**For:** Designers, PMs, and product-minded founders building things people love.

From the foundations of human-centered design and usability, through modern product discovery and process, to craft and prioritization in practice.

Work through these in order:

1. **[The Design of Everyday Things](../kb/cards/design-of-everyday-things.md)** — Don Norman, 1988 (Book, Beginner)  
   The book that defined human-centered design and gave the field its shared language.
2. **[10 Usability Heuristics for User Interface Design](../kb/cards/10-usability-heuristics-for-user-interface-design.md)** — Jakob Nielsen / Nielsen Norman Group, 1994 (Essay / framework, Beginner)  
   Still one of the most portable and field-tested design evaluation frameworks.
3. **[Tim Brown - From Design to Design Thinking](../kb/cards/tim-brown-from-design-to-design-thinking.md)** — Tim Brown, 2009 (Talk, Beginner)  
   It helped define the language of modern product/design collaboration.
4. **[Inventing on Principle](../kb/cards/inventing-on-principle.md)** — Bret Victor, 2012 (Talk, Beginner)  
   An endlessly influential talk on tools, creativity, and doing work that means something.
5. **[Inspired: How to Create Tech Products Customers Love](../kb/cards/inspired-cagan.md)** — Marty Cagan, 2017 (Book, Intermediate)  
   The default reference for modern product management practice.
6. **[Continuous Discovery Habits](../kb/cards/continuous-discovery-habits.md)** — Teresa Torres, 2021 (Book, Intermediate)  
   The clearest public framework for making product discovery continuous rather than occasional.
7. **[Shape Up](../kb/cards/shape-up.md)** — 37signals / Basecamp, 2019 (Book, Intermediate)  
   A useful counter-model for teams that are stuck in bloated roadmaps and endless backlog management.
8. **[RICE: Simple prioritization for product managers](../kb/cards/rice-simple-prioritization-for-product-managers.md)** — Intercom, 2018 (Essay / framework, Beginner)  
   A simple, durable prioritization framework for product roadmaps and startup decision-making.
9. **[Inside Apple Software Design](../kb/cards/inside-apple-software-design.md)** — a16z / Ken Kocienda, 2019 (Podcast interview, Intermediate)  
   A rare primary-source look at software design inside one of the most influential product organizations ever.
